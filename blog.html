Understanding UPI

Unified Payments Interface (UPI) is a real-time payment system developed by the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI). It facilitates instant fund transfer between two bank accounts on a mobile platform without the need for bank details or IFSC codes.

Launched in 2016, UPI has emerged as a popular payment method due to its ease of use, security, and 24x7 availability. It has also played a significant role in promoting digital payments in India.

How does UPI work?

To use UPI, you need to link your bank account to a UPI ID, which is a virtual payment address. Once linked, you can use your UPI ID to initiate transactions and send or receive money instantly.

UPI also supports various payment methods, including:

  • QR code scanning
  • Virtual IDs
  • Mobile number and MMID
  • Account number and IFSC
  • Aadhaar number

UPI transactions can be initiated using a UPI-enabled mobile app, which is available for download on the Google Play Store and Apple App Store. The app allows you to send money, check your balance, and view transaction history, among other features.
